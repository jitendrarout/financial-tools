The Formula — Shown in Full
No black box. This is exactly how the federal estate tax is calculated under IRC §2001 and §2010.
Step-by-Step Calculation
Gross Estate − Debts, Funeral & Administration Costs = Adjusted Gross Estate Adjusted Gross Estate − Marital / Charitable Deduction (if applicable) = Taxable Estate Taxable Estate + Adjusted Taxable Gifts (lifetime gifts over annual exclusion) = Tax Base Tax Base × Tentative Tax Rate (up to 40%) − Applicable Credit Amount (offsets $15M/$30M exemption) = Federal Estate Tax Due + State Estate/Inheritance Tax (if domiciled in a taxing state) = Total Estate Tax Liability
Worked Example

A married couple with a $22,000,000 combined estate, using full portability, shelters $30,000,000 under the 2026 exemption. Taxable estate: $0. Tax owed: $0. But if that same couple owns a $6M vacation property in a state like Oregon (exemption: $1M) titled only in one spouse's name, that state alone could trigger six figures of state-level tax — even though no federal tax is owed.

Strategies That Apply to Your Situation
Generated based on your estate composition. These are the tools sophisticated families and their advisors use to reduce or eliminate estate tax exposure.
Beyond Tax Reduction — Other Advantages of Planning Now
Probate AvoidanceSpeed
Assets properly titled in a revocable or irrevocable trust bypass probate entirely — heirs receive assets in weeks, not the 9–24 months typical of probate court.
PrivacyControl
Wills become public record through probate. Trusts remain private — your family's financial affairs stay out of the courthouse and away from public databases.
Creditor & Divorce ProtectionAsset Protection
Properly structured irrevocable trusts can shield inherited wealth from a beneficiary's creditors, lawsuits, or a future divorcing spouse.
Incapacity PlanningProtection
A funded revocable trust with a successor trustee provision means your affairs are managed seamlessly if you become incapacitated — no court-supervised guardianship needed.
Multi-Generational ControlLegacy
Dynasty trusts and staggered distribution provisions let you dictate how and when heirs receive wealth — protecting against poor decisions at 18, 21, or 25.
Business ContinuitySuccession
Buy-sell agreements funded by life insurance, paired with valuation discounting strategies, prevent forced liquidation of a family business to pay estate taxes.
