The Hidden Cost

You finance everything you buy.

Either you borrow and pay interest to a lender, or you pay cash and give up the interest that money could have earned. There is no third option — every purchase has a financing cost, whether you see it or not.

Over a lifetime, the average household sends a striking share of every earned dollar to banks and finance companies in the form of interest — on mortgages, car loans, credit cards, and business financing. That interest doesn't come back. The Infinite Banking Concept is a strategy to recapture it.

The Mechanism

How Infinite Banking actually works

It isn't a product you buy off a shelf — it's a process built on a specially designed, high-cash-value life insurance policy that you use as your personal banking system.

1

Capitalize a high-cash-value policy

You fund a specially structured dividend-paying whole life policy (the classic IBC vehicle) designed to maximize cash value rather than death benefit. The cash value is liquid, guaranteed to grow, and accessible.

2

Borrow against your cash value — not from it

When you need to finance a purchase, you take a policy loan using your cash value as collateral. Critically, your full cash value keeps growing as if you never touched it, because you borrowed against it rather than withdrawing it.

3

Repay yourself on your own terms

You pay the loan back on a schedule you control — there's no mandatory amortization. The interest you pay flows back toward your policy's value instead of to an outside lender.

4

Recapture and compound, for life

Each cycle, more capital builds in your system. You finance cars, real estate, business needs, and emergencies through your own pool — recapturing interest and compounding cash value across your lifetime, then passing the death benefit tax-free to your heirs.

Bank vs. You

Who profits from your financing?

The same car, the same loan amount, the same interest rate — but a fundamentally different destination for the interest.

Borrowing From a Bank

  • Interest leaves your control permanently
  • The bank earns on money you repay
  • Approval, credit checks, and terms dictated to you
  • Your cash, once spent, stops working for you
  • No residual benefit when the loan is repaid

Becoming Your Own Banker

  • Interest recaptured into your own system
  • Your cash value grows even while borrowed against
  • Guaranteed loan access — no approval, no credit check
  • Capital keeps compounding uninterrupted
  • A growing pool you control for the rest of your life

Real Applications

How families and business owners use it

The strategy is flexible by design. Here's how the same system serves very different financial goals.

DEBT

Eliminating & Recapturing Debt

Instead of paying off high-interest debt and watching that money disappear, you can use policy loans to consolidate debt, then redirect the payments you were making to lenders back into your own system.

Example: A family carrying $40,000 in credit card and auto debt at 9–22% uses policy loans to pay it off, then continues making those same monthly payments — but now to their own policy. The interest that was enriching credit card companies now rebuilds their cash value.
REAL ESTATE

Financing & Down Payments

Real estate investors use policy loans for down payments, renovations, or bridge financing — keeping their cash value compounding while their property generates returns. The same dollar works in two places.

Example: An investor borrows $80,000 against cash value for a rental down payment. The policy continues crediting growth on the full balance, the property cash-flows and appreciates, and the loan is repaid from rental income — a double return on a single pool of capital.
BUSINESS

Funding a Business & Smoothing Cash Flow

Business owners use their policy as a flexible line of capital for inventory, equipment, payroll gaps, or opportunities — without the rigidity of a bank line of credit or the dilution of outside investors.

Example: A business owner finances $50,000 of equipment through a policy loan rather than a bank, controls the repayment schedule around seasonal cash flow, and keeps the interest within their own financial ecosystem.
TAX-FREE INCOME

Supplemental Retirement Income

In retirement, policy loans provide income that isn't reported as taxable income — meaning it doesn't increase your tax bracket, doesn't trigger taxation of Social Security benefits, and doesn't push you over Medicare IRMAA thresholds.

Example: A retiree draws supplemental income via tax-free policy loans in the same years they take 401(k) distributions, blending the two to keep total taxable income in a lower bracket than an all-401(k) strategy would allow.
INVESTMENTS

Opportunity Capital

When an investment opportunity appears, having a liquid, guaranteed-access pool means you can act quickly — financing the opportunity through your system rather than liquidating other assets or missing the window.

Example: An investor keeps cash value as "ready capital." When a discounted opportunity arises, a policy loan funds it within days — no liquidating a brokerage account at a bad time, no realizing capital gains to raise cash.
FAMILY

Major Life Expenses

College tuition, a wedding, a child's first home, a family emergency — instead of student loans or draining savings, the system finances these milestones while the underlying capital keeps growing.

Example: A parent funds four years of college through policy loans instead of student loans, then the (now-graduated) child or the parent repays the system over time — keeping the interest in the family rather than paying a lender.
An Honest Word on Structure

Whole life, IUL, and choosing the right vehicle

The Infinite Banking Concept was built on dividend-paying whole life insurance, and that remains the classic vehicle — its guaranteed cash value and predictable loan provisions make the "banking" mechanism work reliably year after year.

A properly structured Indexed Universal Life (IUL) policy can serve a similar cash-value-banking role with the potential for higher index-linked growth and a 0% market floor — but its cash value is non-guaranteed and depends on crediting rates, caps, and policy charges. Which vehicle fits you depends on your priorities: certainty and stability, or growth potential with more variability.

What we'll always be straight with you about

Infinite Banking is a long-term strategy, not a get-rich-quick scheme. Policies are most effective when funded consistently over many years, and the early years carry higher relative costs before the system hits its stride.

Policy loans accrue interest, and an unmanaged or over-borrowed policy can lapse — which can create a taxable event. Proper structure (avoiding MEC status) and disciplined repayment are essential. This is exactly why working with an advisor who designs the policy correctly matters more than the concept itself.

Common Questions

What clients ask first

Is this the same as "Bank On Yourself"?
"Bank On Yourself" and "Infinite Banking" are two popular names for closely related strategies built on the same foundation: high-cash-value, dividend-paying life insurance used as a personal financing system.
How much do I need to start?
Policies can be designed around a wide range of funding levels. What matters most is consistency and proper structure — we design the policy around what you can reliably commit, not an arbitrary minimum.
What happens if I borrow and don't pay it back?
An outstanding loan balance reduces the death benefit, and if a policy lapses with a large loan outstanding, the gain can become taxable. This is why disciplined repayment and proper policy design are central to the strategy — we'll walk you through exactly how to manage it.
Can I still access my money in an emergency?
Yes — guaranteed access to cash value via policy loans is one of the core advantages, with no application or credit check and typically funds available within days.
