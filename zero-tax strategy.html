The Formula
How "zero or low tax" actually gets calculated, bucket by bucket.
Effective Lifetime Tax Rate by Bucket
Zero-Tax Bucket (Roth, HSA, Muni, Cash-Value Life): Contribution (after-tax or qualifying) + Tax-Free Growth = Tax-Free Withdrawal Tax-Deferred Bucket (401k, Traditional IRA): Pre-Tax Contribution + Tax-Deferred Growth = Withdrawal × Ordinary Income Rate = Tax Owed Later Taxable Bucket (Brokerage): After-Tax Contribution + Growth = Dividends/Realized Gains × Cap Gains Rate = Tax Owed Annually
🌱Before Retirement — Accumulation Phase
The goal here is twofold: lower today's taxable income where it helps, and build the tax-free bucket as large as possible while you have decades of growth ahead of it.
🌳After Retirement — Distribution Phase
The goal shifts to sequencing withdrawals across buckets so you control your bracket every year — instead of the IRS controlling it for you via RMDs.
Without a Bucket Strategy vs. With One
⚠ Without a Plan
− Nearly all savings default into one bucket (usually tax-deferred or plain taxable), with no intentional mix
− Every withdrawal in retirement is taxed at ordinary rates, with no tax-free lever to pull
− RMDs force withdrawals on the IRS's schedule, often at a worse bracket than necessary
− A market downturn early in retirement forces selling taxable assets at the worst possible time
✓ With a Bucket Plan
✓ Three buckets (zero-tax, tax-deferred, taxable) give you a withdrawal lever for any tax environment
✓ You choose your bracket each year by blending sources — instead of one income stream dictating it
✓ Tax-free bucket can be tapped in high-income or market-stress years without moving your bracket
✓ Heirs inherit tax-free or step-up basis assets instead of a fully taxable account
