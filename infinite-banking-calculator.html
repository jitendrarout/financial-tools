The Formula
The participating-loan mechanic, shown explicitly — including the case where it works against you.
Participating Loan Net Position
While borrowed against, per year: Cash Value Credit = Borrowed Balance × Index Rate (0% floor) Loan Interest Cost = Borrowed Balance × Loan Rate Net Spread = Credit − Loan Cost If Index Rate > Loan Rate → positive arbitrage (money works twice) If Index Rate < Loan Rate → negative carry (a flat index year) vs. Bank Financing: Advantage = Interest you would have paid a lender + Net spread on the participating loan

This calculator provides a simplified educational estimate and is not financial, tax, or legal advice. It models a participating (non-direct-recognition) policy loan where borrowed cash value continues to receive index crediting; not all policies or carriers offer this feature, and crediting rates, caps, participation rates, loan charges, and policy expenses vary and are not guaranteed. The 0% floor protects against index losses, but cost of insurance and policy charges still apply in flat years and are not fully reflected here. A negative spread occurs whenever the loan rate exceeds the credited rate. Policy loans reduce cash value and death benefit; lapse with an outstanding loan may create taxable income.
